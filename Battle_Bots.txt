# 🥊 **Battle of the Bots**

### *A Conversational AI-Based Multi-Agent Debate System*

---

## 🔹 **Project Overview**

**Battle of the Bots** is an interactive conversational AI system where multiple language model agents engage in structured debates on a given topic, while another AI agent evaluates their arguments and determines the outcome.

The system simulates a real-world debate using conversational flows:

* One agent argues **in favor (Pro)**
* One agent argues **against (Con)**
* A **Judge agent** evaluates both sides and declares a winner

At its core, the project explores how conversational AI systems can move beyond simple question-answering and participate in **multi-turn, role-driven interactions**.

---

## 🎯 **Objective**

* Build a **multi-agent conversational AI system**
* Simulate structured debates using natural language interaction
* Explore how AI models generate and defend arguments in conversation
* Demonstrate how conversational behavior can be shaped using:

  * Role conditioning (Pro vs Con)
  * Structured prompts
  * Example-driven responses
* Design a system that can later be extended to **fine-tuned conversational models**

---

## 🧠 **Core Concept**

Traditional conversational AI systems are designed for:

* Answering questions
* Assisting users
* Providing information

In **Battle of the Bots**, we extend this idea by enabling:

👉 **AI-to-AI conversation instead of just human-to-AI interaction**

Each agent participates in a **goal-driven conversation**, where:

* Responses are not isolated
* Each message is part of a larger argumentative exchange
* The conversation evolves through multiple turns

This creates a more dynamic and realistic conversational setting.

---

## 🏗️ **System Architecture**

The system consists of three conversational agents:

---

### 🔹 1. Pro Agent

* Engages in conversation by supporting the topic
* Generates structured, persuasive arguments
* Maintains a confident and assertive tone

---

### 🔹 2. Con Agent

* Engages in conversation by opposing the topic
* Provides counterarguments and critiques
* Maintains a skeptical and analytical tone

---

### 🔹 3. Judge Agent

* Acts as a neutral conversational evaluator
* Analyzes both arguments within the dialogue
* Decides the winner and explains the reasoning

---

## 🔄 **Conversational Workflow**

The system operates as a **multi-turn conversational pipeline**:

---

### Step 1: Topic Introduction

A debate question is introduced into the system

---

### Step 2: Initial Responses

* Pro agent presents its argument
* Con agent responds with a counterargument

---

### Step 3: First Evaluation

* Judge analyzes both responses
* Provides an initial decision with reasoning

---

### Step 4: Defense Round (Multi-Turn Interaction)

* Agents respond to each other’s arguments
* Conversation becomes more interactive and dynamic

---

### Step 5: Final Evaluation

* Judge evaluates the full conversation
* Declares final winner or draw

---

### Step 6: Logging & Analysis

* Full conversational transcript is stored
* Used for evaluation and comparison

---

## 🧪 **Dataset Design**

The system uses a structured dataset consisting of:

* Multiple **topics** (e.g., sports, politics, technology)
* Each topic includes:

  * Several **questions**
  * A **Pro argument**
  * A **Con argument**

This dataset helps define how arguments should be structured within a conversational setting and can be used for:

* Simulating trained conversational behavior
* Future fine-tuning of conversational models

---

## ⚙️ **Approach**

### ✅ Prompt-Based Conversational Control

Agents are guided using structured prompts that define:

* Their role (Pro / Con / Judge)
* Their tone and style
* Their response structure

---

### ✅ Few-Shot Conversational Learning

* Example arguments are provided within prompts
* Helps the model mimic realistic conversational patterns

---

### 🔬 **Deep Learning Extension (Planned)**

The system is designed to support:

* Fine-tuning a pre-trained language model on debate-style conversations
* Specializing conversational agents for different roles
* Comparing:

  * Base conversational behavior
  * Adapted (fine-tuned) conversational behavior

---

## 📊 **Evaluation**

The conversational system is evaluated based on:

* Judge decisions (Pro / Con / Draw)
* Quality of arguments within the conversation
* Consistency of each agent’s role
* Ability to respond to opposing viewpoints

---

## 💡 **Key Features**

* Multi-agent conversational AI interaction
* Structured debate-based dialogue
* Role-driven conversation dynamics
* Extendable to deep learning fine-tuning
* Clear and interpretable conversational outputs

---

## 🚀 **Conclusion**

**Battle of the Bots** demonstrates how conversational AI can be extended beyond simple interactions into **dynamic, multi-agent discussions**.

By enabling AI agents to argue, respond, and evaluate within a structured conversational framework, the project highlights the potential of language models in more complex, interactive scenarios — while also laying the groundwork for future deep learning-based improvements.
